Pre-mortem generator asks what would have gone wrong if the research round produced a useless answer, forcing the team to identify round-design failures before they become expensive.

## What it is, how it works

- Targets wrong metrics, wrong scope, unclear audiences, and brittle assumptions.
- Turns failure modes into concrete design checks for the round.
- Is most useful when anchored to the specific structure of the planned work.

## When not to use it

- Do not use it as an automatic verdict on research quality.
- Do not use it before the team agrees how scope decisions should be reviewed.
- Keep human ownership clear when the output affects funding, prioritization, or recommendations.

## How to get started

- Pick one active scope workflow where the cost of a bad call is visible.
- Run the assistant on a small sample and compare its output against human review.
- Decide which outputs become decision inputs and which stay as reviewer prompts.

## Best practices / further reading

- Store outputs with the candidate or round so later teams can audit the reasoning.
- Track whether the tool changed decisions, saved time, or surfaced missed evidence.
- Review results after the round and feed lessons back into the next workflow.
