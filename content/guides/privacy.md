This page summarizes DoneThat's data privacy approach described in [Data privacy measures](https://donethat.ai/data).

DoneThat captures screenshots and activity logs locally, applies local cleaning (including app exclusions), and processes raw inputs in real time without storing raw data.

By default, DoneThat uses privacy-focused LLM processing where raw data is not stored or used for training. You can also bring your own Gemini or OpenAI-compatible model so raw activity data is processed through your provider.

DoneThat stores structured activity data on EU-hosted servers so you can review, edit, delete, and share summaries based on your visibility settings.

You stay in control with pause options, app exclusions, optional redactions, and editable summaries. Sharing is private by default, and organizations can enforce stricter privacy settings such as German Mode.

DoneThat documents security and compliance measures including encryption in transit and at rest, EU processing defaults, vetted subprocessors, and transparency around AI usage.
